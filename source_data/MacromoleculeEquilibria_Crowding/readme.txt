IJBCB
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biocel.2006.05.012
samples: Xanthine oxidase and Cu,Zn-superoxide dismutase - these are two enzymes -homodimers - consist of two monomers
charges: The net charge of Cu,Zn-SOD1 under the experimental conditions (pH 7.4) was taken as$Z \approx -7$per dimer ($\approx -3.5$per monomer), consistent with experimental protein charge-ladder measurements of SOD1 net charge as a function of pH and metal loading, and with prior estimates that wild-type SOD1 carries a net charge of roughly$-6$at neutral pH. https://doi.org/10.1002/pro.2526 and https://doi.org/10.1016/S1876-1623(10)79004-0
Xanthine oxidase net charge at pH 7.4 was treated as slightly negative and near neutral, consistent with reported bovine milk XO isoforms having isoelectric points in the range$pI \approx 6.9\text{–}7.6$, which implies a small magnitude of net charge around pH 7.4. A representative value$Z \approx -2$per monomer (with a plausible range from$0$to about$-5$) was adopted for estimates/simulations as a reasonable modeling assumption consistent with these pI values. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00573841
Rg: The radius of gyration of Cu,Zn-SOD1 under aqueous buffer conditions was taken as$R_g \approx 24\text{ Å}$, consistent with small-angle X-ray scattering (SAXS) measurements on the dimeric wild-type enzyme. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bpj.2014.12.046
When only an order‑of‑magnitude size was required, the radius of gyration of xanthine oxidase was assumed to fall in the range$R_g \approx 4\text{–}6\text{ nm}$, consistent with SAXS benchmark data for globular proteins of comparable molecular mass. https://doi.org/10.1107/S002188980700252X
buffer: 10 mM HEPES, 150 mM NaCl
